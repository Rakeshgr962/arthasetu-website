What is Portfolio Management?

Portfolio management is the art and science of assembling a collection of investments (stocks, bonds, funds, real estate) aligned with your goals, time horizon, and risk appetite. It's about creating a mix that works for you.

üéØ Goals

Define what you're investing for: retirement, home, education, passive income, or wealth building.

üìÖ Time Horizon

How long until you need the money? Longer time = more equity. Shorter time = more debt.

5-Step Portfolio Building Process

Step 1: Define Goals

What are you saving for? Retirement in 20 years? Home in 5 years? Kids' education in 10 years? Each goal has a different timeline and risk profile.

Step 2: Assess Risk

How much volatility can you handle? Take a risk profiler test. Can you sleep at night if your portfolio drops 30% in a crash?

Step 3: Allocate Assets

Build your target allocation. Pick % for equity, bonds, and cash based on your risk profile and time horizon from the table above.

Step 4: Select Investments

Choose funds, stocks, or bonds. Use our Fund Choosing Guide to pick good mutual funds for each category.

Step 5: Review & Rebalance

Monitor annually. If your allocation drifts (e.g., equities grow to 85%), sell some and buy bonds to rebalance to target.

Understanding Portfolio Rebalancing

Over time, some investments will grow faster than others. This means your allocation drifts from your target. Rebalancing is the process of selling overweight assets and buying underweight assets to return to your target allocation.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Drift-Based (Data-Driven)

Pro Tip: Most investors choose annual calendar rebalancing. It's simple, tax-efficient, and effective. Do it every January.

Annual Portfolio Review Checklist

  • Review Performance: How did each investment perform vs benchmark?
  • Check Allocation Drift: Has your equity/debt mix drifted from target?
  • Rebalance if Needed: Sell overweight assets, buy underweight assets
  • Assess Fund Health: Has fund manager changed? Is ER still competitive?
  • Update Goals: Have your financial goals changed? Update your plan accordingly.
  • Tax Planning: Plan tax-loss harvesting or strategic selling for December
  • Insurance Check: Do you still have adequate life, health, and disability cover?
  • Emergency Fund: Is your 6-month emergency fund still intact?

Common Portfolio Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Panic Selling

During market crashes, panic and sell at the worst time. Remember: crashes are buying opportunities for long-term investors.

‚ùå Chasing Performance

Buying funds after they've had amazing returns. By then, you're buying at peak. Stick to your plan.

‚ùå Over-Diversification

Owning 50+ holdings dilutes returns and increases fees. 10-15 well-chosen investments are enough.

‚ùå Ignoring Costs

High expense ratios (2%+) destroy wealth over decades. Always pick low-cost funds.
