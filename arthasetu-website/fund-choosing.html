The 5-Step Framework to Choose the Right Fund

Choosing a fund isn't about finding the best performer‚Äîit's about finding the right fit for your goals and risk appetite. Follow this framework:

1

Category Selection

Based on your time horizon and goals, pick a category: Equity, Balanced, Debt, or Liquid. Longer time = More equity.

2

Long-Term Track Record

Check 3-year, 5-year, and 10-year returns. Consistent performance beats one-year spikes. Look for funds that survived market crashes.

3

Rolling Returns & Consistency

Calculate rolling returns (1-year periods over 5 years). Consistent performers win. Avoid funds with wild swings.

4

Risk Metrics

Compare volatility, Sharpe ratio, and downside behavior. Low ER, positive alpha, and good Sharpe ratio = Better fund.

5

Cost & Fund Hygiene

Lower expense ratios (ER) beat higher ER consistently. Check fund manager tenure and AUM stability.

Key Metrics Explained

Understand the metrics that separate good funds from great ones:

Metric What It Means What to Look For
Expense Ratio (ER)
Annual cost to manage the fund
 How much fund house charges yearly Lower ER (0.5-1.5%) = Better
Alpha
Excess return vs benchmark
 How much the fund beats its benchmark Positive Alpha = Fund manager is skilled
Beta
Volatility vs market
 How much fund swings compared to index Beta = 1 (moves with market), <1 (less volatile)
Sharpe Ratio
Risk-adjusted return
 Return earned per unit of risk taken Higher Sharpe Ratio (2+) = Better risk-adjusted returns
Volatility
Standard deviation of returns
 How much fund price jumps around Lower volatility = Smoother ride, less stress
CAGR
Compounded Annual Growth Rate
 Average yearly growth over 3/5/10 years Consistent CAGR > Declining CAGR
